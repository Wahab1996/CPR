Emergency Algorithms

Status Epilepticus (SE)

  • Airway, Breathing, Circulation
  • Benzodiazepine (e.g., Diazepam IV 0.2-0.5 mg/kg)
  • Repeat once after 5-10 min if seizures persist
  • Fosphenytoin or Levetiracetam IV if no response
  • Consider PICU and intubation

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

  • Start IV fluids (NS 10-20 ml/kg over 1-2 hrs)
  • Start insulin 0.05-0.1 units/kg/hr after 1-2 hrs
  • Monitor glucose, electrolytes, and pH every 1-2 hrs
  • Avoid rapid correction of hyperglycemia

Asthma Exacerbation

  • Oxygen to maintain SpO₂ > 92%
  • SABA (e.g., Salbutamol) via MDI or Nebulizer
  • Oral or IV steroids
  • Reassess frequently, escalate to IV MgSO₄ or aminophylline if needed

Anaphylaxis

  • IM Adrenaline 0.01 mg/kg (max 0.3–0.5 mg)
  • Airway support and high-flow oxygen
  • IV fluids bolus (NS)
  • Antihistamines and corticosteroids

CPR (Pediatric)

  • Check responsiveness and breathing
  • Start chest compressions (rate 100–120/min)
  • Use 15:2 compression-to-breath ratio
  • Attach defibrillator if available
  • Continue cycles and reassess
